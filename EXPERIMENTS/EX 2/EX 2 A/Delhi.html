Welcome to Delhi

Delhi, India's capital territory, is a massive metropolitan area in the country's north. In Old Delhi, a neighborhood dating to the 1600s, stands the imposing Mughal-era Red Fort, a symbol of India, and the sprawling Jama Masjid mosque, whose courtyard accommodates 25,000 people. Nearby is Chandni Chowk, a vibrant bazaar filled with food carts, sweets shops, and spice stalls.

Natural Resources of Delhi

  • Water Resources: The Yamuna River is a significant water resource for Delhi, supporting agriculture, drinking water, and industrial needs.
  • Forests: Despite its urbanization, Delhi has several green belts and forested areas that contribute to its ecological balance.
  • Sand and Gravel: These are important natural resources for the construction industry, which is booming in Delhi.
  • Flora and Fauna: Delhi is home to several species of plants and animals, with parks and wildlife sanctuaries providing habitat.
